2.Newland's Law of Octaves:

In 1865, John Newlands, an English Chemist observed that if the elements were arranged were arranged in the order of their increasing atomic weights,the eighth element starting from a given one, possessed properties similar to the first, like the eighth note in an Octave of Music. He called it the Law of Octaves. It failed in the case of elements with higher atomic weight.
